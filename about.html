Annie Klingenberg

I am a multimedia journalist covering politics as an MSJ candidate for the Medill School of Journalism in D.C. Much of my reporting is focused on the intersection of politics and social justice, especially gender-related issues. I’m looking forward to covering the upcoming 2022 midterm elections. I'm also a recent recipient of a 2022 White House Correspondents' Association scholarship award.

I graduated summa cum laude from the University of Pittsburgh with a Bachelor of Arts in political science and communication. During my undergraduate career, I worked in political communications for a variety of political candidates and organizations. Through those experiences, I learned how to craft social media and speeches that attract and engage audiences.

I am well-versed in both written and video journalism techniques; I hold value and am constantly working on bettering my skills in both text and video formats. I am proficient in Adobe Premiere Pro and have created videos in both documentary and broadcast styles. I'm not afraid of being on camera, but I also enjoy producing content from behind the scenes.

I grew up entrenched in endless campaign efforts as a lifelong Pennsylvanian, so I am constantly drawn toward covering nail-biting elections. I hope to work as a campaign embed in the future and eventually become a White House Correspondent.

Outside of politics, I hold a deep passion for Wawa, regularly attend cycling classes to work out my frustrations, and more than anything else, hope Pitt’s football team will continue to surprise me.
